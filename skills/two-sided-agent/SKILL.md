---
name: two-sided-agent
description: "Arrive at a more accurate answer, plan, or conclusion by spawning two subagents that argue opposite sides: one builds the case for, one builds the case against. Use for discovery, refinement, or verification — whenever a question or result benefits from genuine opposition before you commit."
---

First form your own tentative view. Then spawn two subagents: a defender that argues why the answer is correct, strong, and worth keeping, and an attacker that argues why it is wrong, incomplete, risky, or missing something important. Give both the current state and relevant context; tell them not to spawn further agents and to make concrete, grounded arguments rather than vague opinions. After both report back, compare their claims directly — do not average or split the difference — resolve conflicts by checking evidence, and follow up when a point is weak or unclear. Revise where the attack survives scrutiny, keep where the defense holds, and synthesize into a final conclusion that is honest about what remains uncertain.
